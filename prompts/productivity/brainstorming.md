# Brainstorming Prompts

## Overview

These AI prompts help you generate creative ideas, explore possibilities, and break through mental blocks. Use them to facilitate individual or team brainstorming sessions, develop innovative solutions, and expand your thinking.

## Idea Generation

### Open Brainstorming

**Prompt**: "Help me brainstorm ideas for [TOPIC/PROBLEM]. Generate 20 diverse ideas ranging from conventional to highly creative. For each idea, provide:
- Brief description
- Key benefit
- Implementation difficulty (Easy/Medium/Hard)

Don't filter or judge - embrace wild and unconventional thinking."

### Problem-Solving Brainstorm

**Prompt**: "I'm trying to solve this problem: [PROBLEM DESCRIPTION]

**Context:**
- Current situation
- Constraints
- Goals

Generate 15 potential solutions using different approaches:
- Conventional solutions
- Innovative approaches
- Lateral thinking solutions
- Technology-enabled options
- Low-cost alternatives

For each, explain the core concept and potential impact."

### "What If" Exploration

**Prompt**: "Explore 'what if' scenarios for [SITUATION/PRODUCT/IDEA]:

What if we:
- Removed a key feature?
- Doubled our budget?
- Had to launch in 1 week?
- Served a completely different audience?
- Combined it with [X]?
- Made it 10x bigger/smaller?

For each scenario, describe:
- The modified concept
- Surprising implications
- Unexpected opportunities
- Potential challenges"

## Creative Techniques

### SCAMPER Method

**Prompt**: "Apply the SCAMPER technique to [PRODUCT/SERVICE/IDEA]:

**Substitute**: What elements could be replaced?
**Combine**: What could be merged or integrated?
**Adapt**: What else is like this? What could we copy?
**Modify**: How could we change attributes, color, shape, form?
**Put to another use**: What else could this be used for?
**Eliminate**: What could we remove or simplify?
**Reverse/Rearrange**: What if we did the opposite or changed the sequence?

Provide 3-5 innovative ideas for each category with practical examples."

### Reverse Brainstorming

**Prompt**: "Let's use reverse brainstorming for [GOAL/PROBLEM]:

Instead of 'How can we achieve X?', answer:
'How could we ensure we FAIL at X?'

Generate 10-15 ways to guarantee failure, then:
1. Reverse each failure scenario into a success strategy
2. Identify non-obvious risks we should avoid
3. Highlight unconventional approaches we might miss

This reveals blind spots and unexpected solutions."

## Specific Applications

### Product Innovation

**Prompt**: "Brainstorm innovative features or improvements for [PRODUCT]:

**Current State:**
[Description]

**Target Audience:**
[Audience]

**Competitors:**
[Competitors]

Generate ideas in these categories:
- User experience enhancements
- New capabilities
- Integration opportunities
- Automation possibilities
- Personalization features
- Sustainability improvements

Prioritize by user value and feasibility."

- Case studies
- Tutorials
- Interviews

For each idea, provide:
- Catchy title

Mix evergreen and trending topics."

## Brainstorming Facilitation

### Team Session Starter

**Prompt**: "Create an effective brainstorming session agenda for [TOPIC/CHALLENGE]:

**Session details:**
- Duration: [Time]
- Participants: [Roles]
- Goal: [Objective]

Provide:
1. Warm-up exercise (5-10 min)
2. Problem statement framing
3. Ideation activities (3-4 different techniques)
4. Idea organization method
5. Evaluation criteria
6. Next steps process

Include facilitation tips and expected outputs."

### Idea Building

**Prompt**: "Take this initial idea and expand it through 'Yes, And' brainstorming:

**Initial idea:**
[Idea]

Build upon it 10 times, each time saying 'Yes, and...'
Add layers, combine concepts, exaggerate, or take unexpected turns.

After the chain, identify:
- Most promising evolved ideas
- Unexpected insights
- Elements that could be combined
- Next steps for development"

### Constraint-Based Creativity

**Prompt**: "Generate ideas for [CHALLENGE] with these constraints:

**Constraints:**
- Budget: [Amount]
- Time: [Timeframe]
- Resources: [Available resources]
- Requirements: [Must-haves]

Often constraints spark creativity. Generate 15 ideas that:
- Work within limitations
- Turn constraints into advantages
- Find resourceful solutions
- Identify unconventional resources

Rank by creativity and feasibility."

## Idea Evaluation

### Rapid Filtering

**Prompt**: "I have these brainstormed ideas:

[LIST OF IDEAS]

Help me evaluate them using:

**Impact Potential** (1-10)
**Feasibility** (1-10)
**Originality** (1-10)
**Resource Requirements** (Low/Medium/High)
**Time to Implement** (Short/Medium/Long)

Create a matrix and recommend:
- Top 5 ideas to pursue immediately
- 3 ideas for further research
- Ideas to combine
- Ideas to save for later"

### Concept Development

**Prompt**: "Take this raw brainstormed idea and develop it into a concrete concept:

**Raw idea:**
[Idea]

Develop:
1. **Refined concept**: Clear, compelling description
2. **Key features**: 3-5 core elements
3. **Target audience**: Specific segments
4. **Value proposition**: Why it matters
5. **Differentiation**: What makes it unique
6. **Success metrics**: How to measure impact
7. **Next steps**: Immediate actions
8. **Potential obstacles**: Risks to address"

## Best Practices

1. **Quantity first** - Generate many ideas before evaluating
2. **Defer judgment** - Don't criticize during ideation
3. **Build on others** - Use 'Yes, and...' thinking
4. **Embrace wild ideas** - Crazy ideas can spark practical ones
5. **Be visual** - Sketch, diagram, or map ideas
6. **Change perspective** - Look from different angles
7. **Take breaks** - Let ideas incubate
8. **Combine ideas** - Look for unexpected mashups
9. **Question assumptions** - Challenge what you take for granted
10. **Capture everything** - Don't lose ideas

## Tips for AI Brainstorming

- **Provide context**: The more background, the better ideas
- **Specify quantity**: Ask for specific numbers of ideas
- **Request variety**: Ask for different types or categories
- **Iterate**: Take promising ideas and dig deeper
- **Combine outputs**: Ask AI to merge and develop concepts
- **Challenge AI**: Ask for crazier, bolder, or more specific ideas
