# Your First AI Prompt in Notion

> **Beginner Tutorial** | Estimated time: 10-15 minutes | Prerequisites: Notion account with AI enabled

## Overview

This tutorial walks you through writing your very first AI prompt in Notion. By the end, you will know how to activate Notion AI, write an effective prompt, and use the output productively.

---

## Step 1: Open Notion AI

There are three ways to start Notion AI:

**Option A: The Space Bar Shortcut**
Click anywhere on a blank line in a Notion page, then press the Space bar. A prompt input will appear.

**Option B: The AI Button**
Click the sparkle icon that appears when you hover over any block on a page. Select "Ask AI" from the menu.

**Option C: Keyboard Shortcut**
Press Cmd+J (Mac) or Ctrl+J (Windows) to open the AI prompt from anywhere on the page.

---

## Step 2: Write Your First Prompt

Type this prompt exactly as written (you will customize it later):

> Summarize the key benefits of using a second brain system for personal productivity. Keep it to 3 bullet points.

Press Enter or click the send button. Notion AI will generate a response within a few seconds.

---

## Step 3: Understand the Output

After the AI responds, you have several options:

- **Keep** - Inserts the AI output directly into your page
- **Try Again** - Regenerates a new response to the same prompt
- **Discard** - Cancels and removes the AI response
- **Make it longer / shorter** - Refines the length of the output

Try clicking "Keep" to insert the response into your page.

---

## Step 4: Your First Good Prompt vs. a Weak Prompt

### Weak Prompt:
> Tell me about productivity

This is vague. The AI does not know what aspect of productivity you want, what format to use, or how long the response should be.

### Strong Prompt:
> I am a freelance designer. Write 5 specific tips for managing client projects using Notion. Format as a numbered list with a one-sentence explanation for each tip.

Strong prompts include: **who you are** (context), **what you want** (clear task), **what format to use** (structure), and **any constraints** (length, tone, audience).

---

## Step 5: Try These Beginner Prompts

Practice with these prompts on a new Notion page:

**For summarizing:**
> Summarize the following meeting notes into 3 action items: [paste your notes here]

**For brainstorming:**
> Give me 10 blog post ideas for a small business owner in the health and wellness space.

**For writing:**
> Write a professional bio for a software developer with 5 years of experience who specializes in web apps. Keep it under 100 words.

**For explaining:**
> Explain the concept of compound interest as if I am a 15-year-old. Use a real-life example.

---

## Step 6: Use AI on Existing Content

You can also apply AI to content already on your page:

1. Select (highlight) any text on your Notion page
2. A toolbar will appear — click the sparkle (AI) icon
3. Choose an action: Summarize, Fix spelling and grammar, Make shorter, Make longer, Translate, or Change tone

Try selecting a paragraph you just wrote and clicking "Fix spelling and grammar" or "Make it more professional."

---

## Common Beginner Mistakes

| Mistake | What to Do Instead |
|---|---|
| Prompt is too short or vague | Add context: who, what, format, constraints |
| Using AI for one-word answers | Use AI for creative or analytical tasks where it saves you time |
| Accepting output without reviewing | Always read the output and edit as needed — AI makes mistakes |
| Forgetting to specify format | Say "as a list", "as a table", "in 2 paragraphs", etc. |
