## üéì Lessons Learned

### What Works
1. **Start Small**: Pilot with one workflow before scaling
2. **Human-in-Loop**: AI generates, humans refine and approve
3. **Clear Prompts**: Specific instructions yield better results
4. **Iterative Improvement**: Refine templates and prompts over time

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Over-Automation**: Not everything needs AI
2. **Lack of Training**: Team needs onboarding to use effectively
3. **Quality Assumptions**: Always review AI-generated content
4. **Prompt Vagueness**: Generic prompts = generic results

### Best Practices
- **Define Use Cases**: Identify specific pain points AI can address
- **Establish Guidelines**: When to use AI, when to do manual work
- **Create Templates**: Build reusable prompts for common tasks
- **Measure Impact**: Track time savings and quality metrics
- **Share Learnings**: Document what works for team knowledge

## üîÑ Implementation Framework

### Phase 1: Discovery (Week 1-2)
1. Identify repetitive, time-consuming tasks
2. Map current workflows and pain points
3. Prioritize use cases by impact potential

### Phase 2: Pilot (Week 3-6)
1. Select 1-2 high-impact use cases
2. Build templates and test with small group
3. Collect feedback and iterate

### Phase 3: Scale (Week 7-12)
1. Roll out to broader team/organization
2. Provide training and documentation
3. Monitor adoption and refine

### Phase 4: Optimize (Ongoing)
1. Collect metrics on time savings and quality
2. Identify new use cases
3. Continuously improve prompts and workflows
