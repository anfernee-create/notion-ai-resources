# Personal Knowledge Base for Solopreneurs

Build a second brain in Notion that captures, connects, and surfaces your accumulated knowledge — helping you produce more and better work as a solopreneur.

## Overview

A solopreneur's most valuable asset is their knowledge and expertise. A well-designed Personal Knowledge Base (PKB) in Notion allows you to capture ideas, client insights, market research, and lessons learned — then retrieve and apply that knowledge when you need it most.

## System Components

### 1. Idea Capture Inbox

A friction-free place to dump everything:
- **Title** (Title) — quick description
- **Type** (Select) — Idea, Insight, Resource, Quote, Task
- **Source** (Text) — where it came from
- **Raw Content** (Text) — the actual content
- **AI Summary** (AI) — one-sentence summary
- **Status** (Select) — Inbox / Processed / Archived

### 2. Knowledge Articles

Processed, evergreen knowledge:
- **Topic** (Title)
- **Category** (Multi-select) — Business, Marketing, Technical, etc.
- **Content** (Text) — your synthesized knowledge
- **AI Key Points** (AI) — extracted highlights
- **AI Related Topics** (AI) — connected knowledge areas
- **Sources** (Relation) → Inbox items that fed this article
- **Last Reviewed** (Date)

### 3. Client Insights Database

Track what you learn from each client engagement:
- **Client** (Title)
- **Industry** (Select)
- **Challenges** (Text)
- **Solutions Applied** (Text)
- **AI Pattern Analysis** (AI) — identify patterns across clients
- **Reusable Elements** (Text) — what can be templated

## AI-Powered Features

### Weekly Knowledge Review
Add an AI property to surface forgotten knowledge:
> "Review the knowledge articles tagged [Category] that haven't been accessed in 30+ days. Identify 3 that are most relevant to current active projects."

### Idea Connection Finder
> "Based on this new idea, identify 3-5 existing knowledge articles that connect to or could enhance this concept: {{New Idea}}"

### Client Pattern Recognition
> "Across all client insights in the database, identify the top 3 recurring challenges and the most successful solution patterns."

## Workflow

1. **Capture daily**: Use the inbox for everything
2. **Process weekly**: Move inbox items to knowledge articles
3. **Review monthly**: Use AI to surface forgotten insights
4. **Apply continuously**: Reference PKB before starting any project

## Best Practices

- Capture immediately — don't rely on memory
- Write in your own words, not just quotes
- Link related ideas aggressively
- Keep articles short (300-500 words) and focused
- Review before client calls — surface relevant past insights
